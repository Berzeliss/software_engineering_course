# CLASSIC START-UP ANALYSIS  
## Idea: Simple Study Planner App

> [prompt used: “give me ideas for start-up analysis”]

---

## 1. Company Goals  
The main goal of the start-up is to develop a simple and intuitive digital study planner that helps students organize their academic workload. The application aims to reduce stress caused by poor time management and fragmented planning tools. The focus is on improving productivity through structured task and schedule management.

> [prompt used: “define company goals for a student study planner app”]

---

## 2. Vision  
The long-term vision is to become a widely used student productivity tool that centralizes study planning in one platform. The application should become a reliable daily application for students throughout their academic journey.

> [prompt used: “write a vision for a student study planner startup”]

---

## 3. Preliminary Study  
A preliminary analysis shows that many students currently rely on multiple disconnected tools such as calendars, notebooks, and messaging apps to manage their studies. This leads to inefficiencies, missed deadlines, and poor coordination in group work. Therefore, there is a clear need for a centralized planning solution.

> [prompt used: “what problems do students face when organizing study tasks?”]

---

## 4. Market Analysis  
The market for productivity and planning tools is highly competitive, with established solutions such as Notion, Google Calendar, and Microsoft To Do. However, most of these tools are not specifically designed for academic use cases. This creates an opportunity for a specialized student-focused application that addresses academic workflows more directly.

> [prompt used: “compare Notion, Google Calendar, and other planning tools, compare them to student needs”]

---

## 5. Stakeholders and Interests  
The main stakeholders are students, who are the primary users of the system. Secondary stakeholders include educational institutions, lecturers, and potentially parents. Students are interested in simplicity and efficiency, while institutions may value improved academic performance and organization. These differing interests must be considered in the system design.

> [prompt used: “who are stakeholders in a student study planning app?”]

---

## 6. Requirements Engineering Approach  
Requirements are derived using an assumption-based analysis combined with typical student workflows. In a real project, methods such as interviews or surveys would be used to refine requirements. The goal is to identify functional needs such as task creation, scheduling, and reminder systems, as well as non-functional requirements like usability and performance.

> [prompt used: “list functional requirements and use cases for a study planner app”]

---

## 7. Requirements and Use Cases  
The system must allow users to create tasks, assign deadlines, and organize study schedules. A key use case is a student adding assignments and viewing them in a calendar overview. Another use case is managing group study sessions by sharing tasks with other users.

---

## 8. Business Processes  
The core business process revolves around how students plan their studies. First, tasks are entered into the system, then organized into a schedule, and finally tracked until completion. This process replaces fragmented planning methods such as paper notes or multiple apps.

---

## 9. Feasibility Study and Risk Analysis  
The project is technically feasible using common web or mobile development technologies. However, risks include low user adoption due to competition from established tools and potential lack of engagement if the system is not sufficiently simple. Another risk is overcomplicating the product with unnecessary features.

> [prompt used: “what risks does a student study planner startup face?”]

---

## 10. Specifications  
The system is a digital application consisting of a task management module, calendar view, and notification system. It is designed for mobile and web platforms. The application must support real-time updates and ensure data consistency across devices.

---

## 11. GUI Prototype (not visualized)  
The user interface should be minimalistic and user-friendly, focusing on quick task entry and clear calendar visualization. A prototype would include a dashboard, task list view, and calendar overview.

> [prompt used: “write a GUI prototype for a student study planner app”]
